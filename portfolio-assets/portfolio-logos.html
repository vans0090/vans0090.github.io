MY LOGOS

This logo I made for myself that depicts the initials for "Vini Arts" as well as represent my full name VAnshika. Purple and lavender color is my favorite color, so I added it into my logo. To make a logo for myself was a challenge and a goal for me because I had to come up with something creative yet elegant style. Being not a company, and a person, I wad to keep it more realistic and coping up with the human personality. So I chose the old-school technique of combining two or three letters with some designer strokes in the end to balance with the block letters.

Carny Cones was the name of one of our projects. We build this logo for an ice-cream truck that sells ice-cream in the amusement fairs. We took ice-cream cone obviously as our primary element and on its scoop we decided to add the cartwheel ride to show the importance of the fairs and events where the truck goes. The amusement and joyfulness reflects in this logo along with the colors we chose. The goofiness of the typography also keeps up with the theme. Initials of the Carny Cones in the middle adds as an important design element instead of just adding a small circle. The key message "Take your Taste Buds for a Ride" was really cool and totally matches with the auroma that we wanted to set Around for this logo and the shopkeeper.

This was by far one of my best logos that I created. Suzanne Collins is am American writer and author and I had to redesign her website. I made this logo for her that depicts her initials "S,C" in the form of books as her profession. Two books placed in opposite direction in the stack makes up the 'S' and one single book placed below the stack makes up the 'C'. Little strokes in the books gives the effect of the book pages and her name in the end allows her to place this logo anywhere, either as favicon, app icon, etc.

Lazzy Late was a fun little logo I made for my friend. He was upto starting a new meme page on Instagram and wanted to have a logo. We both came up with this name and I designed this logo for him showing "zz" for snoring instead of just a sigle 'z' in the lazy to excite the audience, alarm clock that is not ringing or stopped to depict as a sign of 'Do Not Disturb' in the morning, and other fun elements.
